What about the "Challenge" part of this?

The challenge is for you to design your own Maker Coin!

  • Rule #1: do not spend any money on software or a 3D printer
  • Rule #2: in total this is a challenging project. There is no rush. Try your best not to get frustrated, stick with it

Many "nerdy hobbyists" like to use Fusion 360. Brian used it for the pill bottle. Angus will walk you thru how to design your Maker Coin in Fusion 360. You may need an adult's help to install Fusion 360 onto a home computer. If you cannot install software on your computer, or if you already know and like TinkerCad this is a perfectly fine alternative

I skipped this part because I had Rutherford the Owl art, but Angus uses InkScape to put a logo on the coin in his tutorial

Ask an adult for help if you need to install software and remember both Rule #1 and #2!

Wrap Up

I hope you enjoyed this presentation, and that it might get you excited to learn more about 3D printing and design!

If you were able to design your Maker Coin and you would like it printed please export an STL format file, change the filename to be your name, and upload it here. I will print it out and mail it to you! At this point I have glitter blue, nebula, candy apple red, transparent neon green, galaxy black, ultramarine transparent blue, orange, and clear filaments on hand. If you feel strongly include the color of your preference in the filename. I hope this print will proudly enter your collection of completed Maker Day projects!

I sincerely wish you all a boring, normal, vaccinated future and I look forward to more Maker Days to come! I think we can use the lessons learned here to design wheels for derby cars. I also hope to follow Brian and Jimmy's examples on resiliency and problem solving. Miss you all, take care.
